Ballad of English Oak

[Ballad of the English Oak] A tree hardly seen in Vancouver Standing singular Planted firmly in a move By some Norwegian visitor A sweet gift that improved My life, my city forever It’s hardy branches refused Storms battering since November Hours spent studying its spring greens In summer months spent climbing In winter, asleep I dream Its fall golden green thriving One day it may plummet But in our hearts it’s at summit

KEVIN H, COPYLEFT 2019-2999
